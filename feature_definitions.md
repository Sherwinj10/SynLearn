1\. Agenesis of Corpus Callosum

Definition: A congenital condition where the corpus callosum (the structure connecting the two cerebral hemispheres) is partially or completely absent.

Clinical note: Leads to developmental delay, poor interhemispheric communication.

Reference: Volpe JJ (2018), Neurology of the Newborn

2\. Airway Obstruction

Definition: Partial or complete blockage of the airway that impairs airflow to the lungs.

Clinical note: Common in craniofacial anomalies (e.g., micrognathia).

Reference: American Thoracic Society

3\. Anxiety

Definition: A psychological state characterized by excessive fear, worry, and physiological arousal.

Reference: DSM-5-TR

4\. Aplasia / Hypoplasia of TMJ

Definition:

Aplasia: Complete absence of temporomandibular joint

Hypoplasia: Underdevelopment of the joint

Clinical note: Affects jaw movement, speech, feeding

Reference: Peterson LJ (2012), Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery

5\. Arched Eyebrows

Definition: Eyebrows with a highly curved or elevated contour, often seen in syndromic conditions.

Reference: Jones KL (Smith’s Recognizable Patterns of Human Malformation, 2013\)

6\. Arterial Aneurysms

Definition: Abnormal localized dilation of an artery due to weakening of the vessel wall.

Reference: Kumar et al., Robbins & Cotran Pathologic Basis of Disease

7\. Aspiration

Definition: Entry of food, liquid, or saliva into the airway below the vocal folds.

Clinical note: Leads to pneumonia risk

Reference: Logemann JA (1998), Evaluation and Treatment of Swallowing Disorders

8\. Asymmetric Short Fingers

Definition: Unequal shortening of fingers on one or both hands due to skeletal developmental anomalies.

Reference: Moore KL, The Developing Human

9\. Atrial Septal Defect (ASD)

Definition: A congenital heart defect characterized by an opening in the atrial septum, allowing blood flow between atria.

Reference: Nelson Textbook of Pediatrics

10\. Beaked Nose

Definition: A nose with a prominent, curved bridge resembling a beak.

Reference: Smith DW, Recognizable Patterns of Human Malformation

11\. Behavioral Issues

Definition: Patterns of behavior that are socially inappropriate, disruptive, or maladaptive.

Reference: Mash & Barkley (2014), Child Psychopathology

12\. Bifid Uvula

Definition: A split or cleft uvula, often indicating submucous cleft palate.

Reference: Kummer AW (2014), Cleft Palate and Craniofacial Conditions

13\. Bipolar Disorder

Definition: A mood disorder with episodes of mania/hypomania and depression.

Reference: DSM-5-TR

14\. Brain Anomalies

Definition: Structural abnormalities in the brain due to abnormal development or injury.

Reference: Volpe JJ (2018), Neurology of the Newborn

15\. Broad Thumbs

Definition: Thumbs that are wider than normal due to skeletal variation.

Reference: Jones KL (2013), Recognizable Patterns

16\. Cardiac Defects

Definition: Structural abnormalities of the heart present at birth (congenital heart disease).

Reference: Nelson Textbook of Pediatrics

17\. Cat-like Cry

Definition: A high-pitched, monotonic cry resembling a cat, classically seen in Cri-du-chat syndrome.

Reference: Gorlin RJ (2001), Syndromes of the Head and Neck

18\. Cervical Spine Anomalies

Definition: Structural abnormalities of the neck vertebrae.

Reference: Moore KL, Clinically Oriented Anatomy

19\. Clinodactyly

Definition: Curvature of a finger, typically the fifth digit, in the radial or ulnar direction.

Reference: Jones KL (2013)

20\. Club Foot

Definition: A congenital deformity where the foot is twisted inward and downward.

Reference: Nelson Textbook of Pediatrics

21\. Coarse Facial Features

Definition: Thickened or heavy facial appearance due to soft tissue or skeletal changes.

Reference: Smith DW (1970)

22\. Coloboma

Definition: A congenital defect resulting in missing tissue in structures of the eye or eyelid.

Reference: Kanski JJ (Clinical Ophthalmology)

23\. Conductive Hearing Loss

Definition: Hearing loss due to impaired transmission of sound through outer or middle ear.

Reference: Katz Handbook of Clinical Audiology

24\. Conotruncal Defect

Definition: A group of heart defects involving outflow tracts of the heart (e.g., Tetralogy of Fallot).

Reference: Hoffman JI (2002), Circulation Journal

25\. Coronal Craniosynostosis

Definition: Premature fusion of the coronal suture of the skull, leading to abnormal head shape.

Reference: Cohen MM (2000), Craniosynostosis: Diagnosis, Evaluation and Management

26\. Craniosynostosis

Definition: Premature fusion of one or more cranial sutures, leading to abnormal skull growth and shape.

Reference: Cohen MM (2000), Craniosynostosis: Diagnosis, Evaluation and Management

27\. Cryptorchidism

Definition: Failure of one or both testes to descend into the scrotum.

Reference: Nelson Textbook of Pediatrics

28\. Decreased Enamel

Definition: Reduced thickness or quality of tooth enamel, making teeth prone to decay.

Reference: Neville BW (2015), Oral and Maxillofacial Pathology

29\. Depressed Nasal Tip

Definition: A nasal tip that appears flattened or positioned lower than normal.

Reference: Jones KL (2013), Recognizable Patterns of Human Malformation

30\. Developmental Delay

Definition: Slower-than-expected achievement of motor, cognitive, language, or social milestones.

Reference: American Academy of Pediatrics

31\. Distractibility

Definition: Reduced ability to maintain attention, with easy diversion by external stimuli.

Reference: Barkley RA (2015), Attention-Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder

32\. Downward Slanting Palpebral Fissures

Definition: The outer corners of the eyes are positioned lower than the inner corners.

Reference: Jones KL (2013)

33\. Drooping Eyelids (Ptosis)

Definition: Abnormal drooping of the upper eyelid due to muscle or nerve dysfunction.

Reference: Kanski JJ, Clinical Ophthalmology

34\. Dysmorphic Facial Features

Definition: Facial characteristics that deviate from typical morphology, often indicating genetic conditions.

Reference: Smith DW, Recognizable Patterns of Human Malformation

35\. Ear Anomalies

Definition: Structural abnormalities of the external, middle, or inner ear.

Reference: Moore KL, The Developing Human

36\. Encephalocele

Definition: Neural tube defect where brain tissue protrudes through a skull defect.

Reference: Nelson Textbook of Pediatrics

37\. Endocrine Abnormalities

Definition: Disorders involving hormone production or regulation.

Reference: Melmed S (2016), Williams Textbook of Endocrinology

38\. Epibulbar Lipodermoids

Definition: Benign growths of fatty tissue on the surface of the eye (conjunctiva).

Reference: Kanski JJ, Clinical Ophthalmology

39\. Epicanthal Folds

Definition: Skin folds covering the inner corner (medial canthus) of the eyes.

Reference: Jones KL (2013)

40\. Exophthalmos

Definition: Abnormal protrusion of the eyeballs from the orbit.

Reference: Kanski JJ

41\. Facial Asymmetry

Definition: Unequal size, shape, or position of facial structures on either side.

Reference: Proffit WR (2018), Contemporary Orthodontics

42\. Feeding Difficulty

Definition: Impairment in the ability to suck, chew, or swallow effectively.

Reference: Arvedson JC (2008), Pediatric Swallowing and Feeding

43\. Flat Face

Definition: Reduced forward projection of facial structures, especially midface region.

Reference: Jones KL (2013)

44\. Flat Facial Profile

Definition: Lack of normal convexity when viewed from the side due to midface retrusion.

Reference: Proffit WR (2018)

45\. Flat Nasal Bridge

Definition: Reduced height of the nasal bridge, giving a flattened appearance.

Reference: Jones KL (2013)

46\. Flat Philtrum

Definition: Absence or reduction of the vertical groove between nose and upper lip.

Reference: Astley SJ (2004), Fetal Alcohol Syndrome Diagnostic Guide

47\. Frequent Middle Ear Anomalies

Definition: Recurrent or structural abnormalities affecting the middle ear system.

Reference: Katz Handbook of Clinical Audiology

48\. Genital Anomalies

Definition: Structural abnormalities of the external or internal genital organs.

Reference: Nelson Textbook of Pediatrics

49\. Glossoptosis

Definition: Downward and posterior displacement of the tongue, often causing airway obstruction.

Reference: Kummer AW (2014), Cleft Palate and Craniofacial Conditions

50\. Growth Retardation

Definition: Slower-than-normal physical growth resulting in below-average height/weight for age.

Reference: World Health Organization

51\. Hearing Loss (Conductive / Sensorineural / High-frequency)

Definition: Partial or complete inability to hear due to:

Conductive: outer/middle ear issues

Sensorineural: inner ear/auditory nerve damage

High-frequency: loss in higher pitch perception

Reference: Katz Handbook of Clinical Audiology

52\. Hemihypertrophy

Definition: Asymmetric overgrowth of one side of the body or a body part.

Reference: Nelson Textbook of Pediatrics

53\. Holoprosencephaly

Definition: A developmental brain disorder where the forebrain fails to divide into two hemispheres.

Reference: Volpe JJ (2018), Neurology of the Newborn

54\. Hooded Eyes

Definition: Excess skin folds over the upper eyelids, partially covering them.

Reference: Jones KL (2013)

55\. Hyperactivity

Definition: Excessive motor activity such as restlessness, fidgeting, or inability to stay still.

Reference: DSM-5-TR

56\. Hypernasality

Definition: Excessive nasal resonance during speech due to velopharyngeal insufficiency.

Reference: Kummer AW (2014), Cleft Palate and Craniofacial Conditions

57\. Hypertelorism

Definition: Increased distance between two body parts, typically the eyes (orbital spacing).

Reference: Jones KL (2013)

58\. Hypocalcemia

Definition: Abnormally low levels of calcium in the blood.

Reference: Nelson Textbook of Pediatrics

59\. Hypoplasia (Malar / Maxillary / Mandibular / Midface)

Definition: Underdevelopment or incomplete development of facial bones or structures.

Reference: Proffit WR (2018), Contemporary Orthodontics

60\. Hypotonia

Definition: Decreased muscle tone resulting in floppiness and reduced resistance to movement.

Reference: Nelson Textbook of Pediatrics

61\. Hypospadias

Definition: A congenital condition where the urethral opening is located on the underside of the penis.

Reference: Nelson Textbook of Pediatrics

62\. Immune Deficiency

Definition: Impaired function of the immune system, leading to increased susceptibility to infections.

Reference: Abbas AK (2018), Basic Immunology

63\. Intellectual Disability

Definition: Significant limitations in intellectual functioning and adaptive behavior, with onset during developmental period.

Reference: DSM-5-TR

64\. Joint Hypermobility

Reference: Beighton P (1973), Annals of Rheumatic Diseases

65\. Language Delay / Disorder

Definition: Delayed or impaired development of language comprehension and/or expression.

Reference: American Speech-Language-Hearing Association

66\. Learning Disability

Definition: A neurodevelopmental disorder affecting academic skills like reading, writing, or math.

Reference: DSM-5-TR

67\. Limb Anomalies

Definition: Structural abnormalities of the arms or legs due to developmental defects.

Reference: Moore KL, The Developing Human

68\. Long Face

Reference: Proffit WR (2018)

69\. Long Limbs

Definition: Disproportionately elongated arms and legs relative to body size.

Reference: Nelson Textbook of Pediatrics

70\. Macrostomia

71\. Macroglossia

72\. Malar Hypoplasia

73\. Mandibular Hypoplasia

Reference: Proffit WR (2018)

74\. Microcephaly

75\. Micrognathia

76\. Micropenis

77\. Microphthalmia

Definition: Abnormally small eyes due to incomplete ocular development.

78\. Microtia

79\. Mild Psychosocial Issues
